# Format: Microsite / Landing Page

Web-native HTML landing pages designed for a specific account or audience. CTA-driven, scroll-based narrative, may be shared via direct link.

**Skills that produce this format:** microsite

These principles supplement the universal visual rules in `presentation-principles.md` (one directory up). The universal principles are the floor; these add format-specific requirements.

## Principles

1. **Hero section hooks in 5 seconds.** The above-the-fold content must communicate who this is for and why they should keep reading. Value proposition, not feature list. If the reader doesn't know why they should care by the time they see the first scroll indicator, the hero failed.

2. **CTA-driven design.** Every section ladders toward a call to action. The reader should always feel a gentle pull toward the next step (book a meeting, see a demo, read more). CTAs appear above the fold and after major proof sections.

3. **Scroll-based narrative arc.** The page tells a story through scroll: problem → solution → proof → action. Each scroll-section advances the argument. The reader should feel pulled downward, not pushed.

4. **Responsive and mobile-aware.** Microsites may be viewed on phones. Layouts must stack cleanly on narrow viewports (test at 375px). Touch targets must be tap-friendly. No horizontal scroll on any device.

5. **Light background default.** Microsites aimed at external audiences typically use light backgrounds for professional credibility. Dark themes are reserved for specific brand contexts where the audience expects it.

6. **Social proof is structural.** Testimonials, logos, stats, and case references aren't decoration: they're load-bearing elements that build credibility. Place them where doubt is highest in the narrative arc.

7. **Interactive elements reveal complexity.** Accordions, expandable sections, and hover reveals manage density without overwhelming. Use them to let the reader go deeper on what interests them, not to hide essential content.

8. **Personalization is visible.** If built for a specific account, the personalization should be obvious: their name, their industry context, their challenges. Generic-feeling microsites miss the point of ABM.

9. **Fast and self-contained.** No external JS frameworks, no heavy assets. The page should load instantly. All CSS/JS inline. Images as data URIs or inline SVGs.

## Review Checklist

Format-specific audit for microsites and landing pages. Run alongside the universal presentation checklist.

- [ ] **Hero hooks fast.** Above-the-fold content communicates value proposition clearly. No scroll required to understand what this is about.
- [ ] **CTA present and repeated.** At least one CTA above the fold. Additional CTAs after major proof sections.
- [ ] **Narrative arc.** Sections follow a logical progression (problem → solution → proof → action or similar).
- [ ] **Responsive.** Content stacks cleanly at 375px viewport width. No horizontal scroll on mobile. Touch targets >= 44px.
- [ ] **Social proof present.** At least one form of external validation (quote, logo, stat, case reference).
- [ ] **Personalization visible.** Company name, industry context, or specific challenges referenced, not generic boilerplate.
- [ ] **Self-contained.** No external CSS/JS dependencies beyond Google Fonts. All assets inline.
- [ ] **Print works.** Readable when printed, even if simplified. Interactive elements flatten.
