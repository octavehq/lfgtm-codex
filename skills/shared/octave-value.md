# Octave Value: What Makes the Output Different

Octave workspaces contain your team's opinionated GTM strategy: how you position to different personas, what objections you pre-handle, which proof points you lead with, and how you frame competitive matchups. This is Type 2 context -- not raw facts, but the curated strategic layer that tells an agent how to interpret facts and what to do with them. Skills produce output grounded in that strategy and validated by real field data. The result is something generic AI cannot replicate.

Generic AI knowledge is the fallback, not the default.

## Three Layers, Not a Ranked List

Octave data isn't a priority queue where one source overrides another. It's three layers that work together:

**The Library (strategic layer):** The team's positioning decisions. Personas, segments, competitors, objections, use cases, proof points, Motion ICP cell narratives. This is the backbone. It shapes how everything else gets framed. Field data and enrichment refine and personalize it; they don't replace it.

**Field Data (validation layer):** What's actually happening in conversations and deals. Findings from calls and emails, Learning Loop learnings, deal outcomes. This layer confirms whether the library's positioning is landing, surfaces language that resonates, and exposes gaps between strategy and reality. When field data contradicts the library, surface the tension rather than silently picking one.

**Enrichment (personalization layer):** Third-party intelligence about the specific company or person. Size, funding, tech stack, headcount trends, role trajectory. This layer tailors library positioning and field patterns to the account you're actually talking to.

All three layers are Octave data. The only true fallback is general AI knowledge, and that should be flagged when used.

## The Test

If you strip the Octave data and the output still reads the same, it's not grounded enough. The workspace data should be load-bearing, not decorative.

| Grounded (right) | Generic (wrong) |
|---|---|
| "In 6 of your last 8 enterprise calls, prospects raised implementation timeline before pricing. Lead with your 4-week deployment methodology." | "Enterprise buyers care about implementation timelines. Consider addressing this early in conversations." |
| "VP Engineering personas in mid-market respond to 'single pane of glass' framing 3x more than 'unified platform.'" | "Position your product as a unified solution that simplifies operations." |
| "No proof points match Acme's industry (construction). Closest reference is BuildTech (manufacturing), which shares similar compliance requirements." | "Your solution helps companies in the construction industry improve compliance." |
